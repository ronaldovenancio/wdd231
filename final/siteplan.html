Site Name

MathStarsCMR Brazil

The site name MathStarsCMR Brazil was chosen to represent Brazilian students who excel in national and regional mathematics competitions such as OBM, OBMEP, and OMU. The term "MathStars" conveys the idea of excellence, talent, and prominence—young stars shining through mathematics. The word "Brazil" provides the geographic and national context for the project.

Overview

Purpose

The Mathematics is a science extremely important for everyone and your Olympic Math is one of the most prestigious science olympic competition in the world. In Brazil, there are many different of Olympics Mathematics for specifics level of students, like OBM, OBMEP and OMU.

Audience

This is a competition for students in the 6th to 9th year of elementary school and all grades of high school in public and private schools in Brazil. The competition consists of a test carried out at poles defined by the coordination in a single phase or in two phases.

This guide has the intention to help secondary and high schools students that search about Olympics Mathematics Competition in Brazil to plan your studies in order to participate in some Olympics Mathematics.

Home page

The Mathematics Olympiad is an extension activity carried out, in general, by the Mathematics Department of Federal Universities (UFs) with the support of the Brazilian Mathematics Olympiad Association (AOBM) and STONE, together with other Higher Education Institutions (IES).

The Brazilian Mathematics Society organized the first Mathematics Olympiad (OBM) in 1979. Over the years, OBM has undergone several changes in its format, but maintained the central idea, which is to encourage the study of Mathematics in students, develop and improve teacher training, influence the improvement of teaching, in addition to discovering young people talented.

The Brazilian Mathematics Olympiad (OBM) is a program of the Brazilian Mathematics Society (SBM) and the Brazilian Mathematics Olympiad Association (AOBM). In addition to holding the annual edition of the Olympics throughout Brazil, the OBM organizing committee is responsible for selecting and training Brazilian teams that participate in international competitions, such as the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO), Ibero-American Mathematics Olympiad (IMO) and International Mathematics Competition for University Students (IMC).

Public and private schools can enroll students in the Brazilian Public School Mathematics Olympiads (Obmep), held by the Institute of Pure and Applied Mathematics (Impa), which is linked to the Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation. Obmep is the largest scientific competition in the country and brings together, every year, more than 18 million students from the 6th year of Elementary School to the 3rd year of High School from all states and the Federal District.

According to the director of Impa, Obmep makes an invaluable social and educational contribution. “Especially in needier areas of the country, the Olympics opens up opportunities for personal and professional growth because a medal or an honorable mention represents an opportunity for many of these children to change their lives”, he highlighted.

Medals - This year, OBMEP multiplied the number of medals for those registered. 650 gold, 1,950 silver and 5,850 bronze medals will be distributed to participants from public and private units. Students who win these medals will be invited to participate in the Jr. Scientific Initiation Program (PIC) as an incentive and promotion of academic development. In addition to the increase in medals, the big news in this edition is the regional awards. There will be at least 20,500 recipients and the number of medals will be calculated according to the performance of each federative unit. The regional award will be the responsibility of the local coordination and does not allow access to the Junior Initiation Program.

“This increase aims to stimulate student participation, encourage the study of the subject and improve mathematics teaching in Brazil. This is our main objective”, said the general coordinator of Obmep, Claudio Landim.

In 2023, Brazil ranked 16th in the general ranking of the 64th International Mathematics Olympiad (IMO), the largest mathematical competition at student level. The Brazilian team won 6 medals: 1 gold, 2 silver and 3 bronze. The competition took place on the 8th and 9th of July, in the city of Chiba in Japan.

For IMPA's deputy director, Claudio Landim, general coordinator of OBMEP (Brazilian Public School Mathematics Olympiad), the team's result was an important achievement. “It is important to highlight that all the countries in the top 10 offer intensive training for their teams, which does not occur in Brazil, where training for scientific research is prioritized as opposed to preparation for the Olympics.”

IMO has been held since 1959 and awards young high school students from more than 100 countries. Brazil joined the scientific competition in 1979 and has already won 158 medals, 14 gold, 55 silver and 89 bronze, in addition to 35 honorable mentions.

In 2022, the Brazilian team ranked 19th in the world, with two gold medals – an unprecedented performance for Brazil at the Olympics - in addition to one silver, two bronze and one honorable mention.
