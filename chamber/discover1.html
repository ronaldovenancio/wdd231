Mark Zero Recife

Marco Zero, in Praça Rio Branco, is known as the founding site of the city of Recife and also as the starting point for counting distances calculated from the city.

The place is one of the most important points in the capital of Pernambuco, as it is also a region with strong movement during Carnival.

Pernambuco Arena Stadium

The Itaipava Arena Pernambuco, in São Lourenço da Mata, Metropolitan Region of Recife, was designed within the multipurpose concept already established internationally.

The Itaipava Arena Pernambuco project favors an inverted architecture. Instead of the usual elevated construction solution with a vertical projection, the Arena has its limits softened through an inclined and smooth transition that extends outwards, blending the building with the land itself. The building becomes part of the local topography, avoiding greater impact on the environment. The stadium has 4,700 parking spaces, of which 4,500 are for light vehicles and 200 for buses.

Catamarã Tour in Capibaribe River

Wonderful tour along the waters of the Capibaribe River, covering the three Islands in the Center of Recife (Santo Antônio, Recife Antigo and Boa Vista) passing under Ponte do Limeiro, Ponte Princesa Isabel and Ponte Duarte Coelho.

On the tour, visitors enjoy beautiful landscapes of various tourist attractions such as Praça do Marco Zero, the Ginásio Pernambucano, the Legislative Assembly, the Santa Isabel Theater and the houses on Rua da Aurora. On board, we have the presence of a guide who tells stories and curiosities about Recife.

Come and discover Brazilian Venice on the traditional Catamaran tour.

Francisco Brennand Oficina

The history of Oficina Brennand began in 1971 in the ruins of Cerâmica São João da Várzea, a pottery from the beginning of the 20th century. As a result, what was an old brick and tile factory inherited from master Francisco Brennand's father became his large studio and is now a monumental architectural complex of great originality. As a result, the work is associated with architecture to give shape to Brennand's incomparable universe of art.

So, get ready to find art in every square meter of the 15 km² built area of Oficina Brennand. In other words, there are galleries with varied exhibitions, outdoor sculptures, unique pieces, ornate ceramics, fountains and lots of history.

Brennand Castle, Varzea-Recife

The Ricardo Brennand Institute, in Recife, houses one of the most important artistic and historical collections in the country. The 80 thousand square meter architectural complex is the greatest legacy left by the businessman and patron from Pernambuco.

The Ricardo Brennand Institute (IRB), an architectural complex opened in 2002, occupies an area of almost 80 thousand square meters in the Várzea neighborhood, in Recife. The first thing that catches the attention of the thousands of tourists and students who visit the place every year is the number of works of art displayed in the entrance garden, which houses statues and sculptures. The collection's ground zero is the castle-shaped building, the Weapons Museum.
